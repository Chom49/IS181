As we see in the video Dar es Salaam is grappled with flooding issues. Intense rainfall inundated the city, overwhelming its drainage systems. The low-lying coastal areas and numerous rivers exacerbated the situation, leading to widespread waterlogging in many neighborhoods. The rapid urbanization of Dar es Salaam had intensified the problem. Poorly planned land development and inadequate infrastructure exacerbated the impact of heavy rains. Solid waste and informal settlements obstructed waterways, worsening the flooding. Each downpour posed significant risks: properties were damaged, residents were displaced, and transportation and public services ground to a halt. Furthermore, the increased risk of waterborne diseases loomed over the population, threatening public health.

As the picture shown on the right side ,General Francis Omondi Ogolla, the Chief of the Kenya Defence Forces (KDF), tragically lost his life in a helicopter crash in the western part of Kenya. The incident occurred in Elgeyo Marakwet county, approximately 400 kilometers (250 miles) northwest of the capital, Nairobi. The ill-fated helicopter was carrying a total of 11 military personnel, including General Ogolla. Regrettably, only two individuals survived the crash1. General Ogolla was a gallant officer who had dedicated his life to serving his country.
